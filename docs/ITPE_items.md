# ITPE Items: Goals and Key Concepts

## Major Category 1: Corporate and Legal Affairs
### Middle Category 1: Corporate Activities
#### Item 1: Management and organization theory
- **Goal:** Understand the fundamental concepts about corporate activities and business management.
- **Key Concepts:**
  - **(1) Corporate activities and management resources**
    - **(a) Corporate activities:** Management principles (corporate philosophy), shareholder meeting, closing account, CSR (Corporate Social Responsibility), disclosure, audit, green IT
    - **(b) Management resources:** Stakeholder, corporate brand, work life balance, diversity, OJT, Off-JT, coaching, mentoring, CDP (Career Development Program), mental health
  - **(2) Business management:** Business objectives; financial affairs, property, human resources, information management; PDCA (Plan, Do, Check, and Act), BCP (Business Continuity Plan), BCM (Business Continuity Management), risk assessment, MBO (Management By Objectives), HRM (Human Resource Management), talent management
  - **(3) Management organization:** Hierarchical organization, divisional organization, functional organizations, matrix organization, company system, project organization, in-house company, CEO (Chief Executive Officer), CIO (Chief Information Officer)

#### Item 2: OR and IE
- **Goal:** Analyze familiar business tasks, and understand and utilize typical techniques for solving problems. Understand practical methods of how to visually describe business tasks and utilize them.
- **Key Concepts:**
  - **(1) Understanding business tasks:** Workflow diagram
  - **(2) Job analysis and operational planning:** Pareto chart, ABC analysis, PERT (arrow diagram), critical path method, scatter diagram, radar chart, control chart, histogram, regression analysis
  - **(3) Decision making:** Cause and effect diagram (fishbone diagram), simulation, inventory control, credit control, ordering method
  - **(4) Problem-solving techniques:** Brainstorming, decision tree, affinity diagram

#### Item 3: Accounting and financial affairs
- **Goal:** Understand the fundamental concepts of accounting and financial affairs for corporate activities and business management.
- **Key Concepts:**
  - **(1) Accounting and financial affairs**
    - **(a) Relationship between sales and profits:** Profit, gross profit, operating profit, break-even point, cost, variable cost, fixed cost, volume of sales, variable expense ratio
    - **(b) Types and roles of financial statements:** Balance sheet, cash flow statement, assets (net assets, current assets, fixed assets, deferred assets, tangible assets, intangible assets), liabilities (current liabilities, fixed liabilities), current ratio, profitability, return on investment

### Middle Category 2: Legal Affairs
#### Item 4: Intellectual property rights
- **Goal:** Understand the types of intellectual property rights, the rights that should be protected by law, and the kinds of actions that are illegal.
- **Key Concepts:**
  - **(1) Copyright Act:** Intellectual creations (music, movies, computer programs)
  - **(2) Laws on industrial property rights:** Patent Act, business model patent, Utility Model Act, Design Act, Trademark Act, trademark, service mark
  - **(3) Unfair Competition Prevention Act:** Trade secrets
  - **(4) Software license:** License agreement, open source software, free software, public domain software
  - **(5) Other rights:** Rights of portrait, rights of publicity

#### Item 5: Laws on security
- **Goal:** Understand the outline of typical laws on security.
- **Key Concepts:**
  - **(1) Basic Act on Cyber Security**
  - **(2) Act on the Prohibition of Unauthorized Computer Access**

#### Item 6: Laws on labor and transaction
- **Goal:** Understand the outline of familiar laws on labor and transaction.
- **Key Concepts:**
  - **(1) Laws on labor:** Labor Contract Act, Labor Standards Act, Act for Securing the Proper Operation of Worker Dispatching Undertakings
    - **(a) Labor Standards Act:** Minimum wages, overtime work wages, working hours
    - **(b) Worker Dispatching Act:** License required to dispatch workers
    - **(c) Nondisclosure agreement:** Trade secrets
    - **(d) Contract types:** Service contract, dispatch contract, (quasi-)mandate contract, employment contract, Flextime, free time system
  - **(2) Laws on transaction:** Act against Delay in Payment of Subcontract Proceeds, Etc. to Subcontractors, Product Liability Act, Act on Specified Commercial Transactions

#### Item 7: Other laws, guidelines, and engineer ethics
- **Goal:** Understand the concepts about the norms of companies and engineer ethics. Understand the fundamental concepts of information disclosure requests to governmental agencies.
- **Key Concepts:**
  - **(1) Compliance**
    - **(a) Act on the Protection of Personal Information:** Protection of personal information, duty regulations
    - **(b) Various standards:** Standards of Measures against Computer Viruses, Standards of Measures against Unauthorized Access to Computers, System Management Standards, Information security countermeasure guidelines, Information Security Management Standards
    - **(c) Information ethics:** Intellectual property, personal information, privacy, morals, netiquette, Act on the Limitation of Liability for Damages of Specified Telecommunications Service Providers
  - **(2) Corporate governance:** Whistleblower Protection Act, internal control reporting system
  - **(3) Information disclosure requests to governmental agencies:** Act on Access to Information Held by Administrative Organs

#### Item 8: Standardization
- **Goal:** Understand the significance of standardization.
- **Key Concepts:**
  - **(1) Standardization:** De facto standard
  - **(2) Examples of the standardization in IT:** Bar code, JAN (Japanese Article Number) code, QR Code
  - **(3) Standardization organizations and specifications:** ISO (International Organization for Standardization), IEC (International Electrotechnical Commission), IEEE (Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers), W3C (World Wide Web Consortium), JIS (Japanese Industrial Standards), ISO9000 (quality management system), ISO14000 (environmental management system), ISO/IEC 27000 (information security management system)

- **Key Concepts:**

  - **(2) Terms on business strategy:** Competitive superiority, innovation, core competence, niche strategy, alliance, outsourcing, M&A (mergers and acquisitions), OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturer), fables, franchise chain, MBO (Management Buy-Out), TOB (Take-Over Bid), scale economy, experience curve, vertical integration, commoditization, benchmarking, logistics
  - **(3) Using office tools:** Spreadsheet software, database software, presentation software

#### Item 10: Marketing
- **Goal:** Understand the fundamental concepts relevant to marketing.
- **Key Concepts:**
  - **(1) Basics of marketing:** Marketing research, sales; product and purchase plan, sales promotion, customer satisfaction, UX (User Experience), 4P, 4C, RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) analysis, Ansoff growth matrix, opinion leader, segment marketing, direct marketing, omnichannel, search advertising, push strategy, brand strategy, product life cycle, positioning

#### Item 11: Business strategy and goal/evaluation
- **Goal:** Understand the typical information analysis techniques for planning business strategies.
- **Key Concepts:**
  - **(1) Information analysis techniques for business strategy planning and evaluation:** Mission (corporate philosophy), visions, BSC (balanced score card), CSF (critical success factors), KGI (Key Goal Indicator), KPI (Key Performance Indicator), value engineering

#### Item 12: Business management system
- **Goal:** Understand the fundamental concepts of the business management system.
- **Key Concepts:**
  - **(1) Business management system:** CRM (customer relationship management), value chain management, SCM (supply chain management), TQC (Total Quality Control), TQM (Total Quality Management), ERP (Enterprise Resource Planning) package, six sigma, knowledge management, TOC (Theory Of Constraints)

### Middle Category 4: Technological Strategy Management
#### Item 13: Planning of technology development strategy and technology development plan
- **Goal:** Understand the significance and the purpose of technology development strategies.
- **Key Concepts:**
  - **(1) Technology development strategy and technology development plan:** MOT (Management Of Technology), technology portfolio, patent strategy, technology estimate method, process innovation, product innovation, road map

### Middle Category 5: Business Industry
#### Item 14: Business system
- **Goal:** Understand the characteristics of typical systems in various business fields.
- **Key Concepts:**
  - **(1) Systems in typical business fields:** Distribution information system, financial information system, POS (point of sales) system, GPS, GIS, ETC system, IC card, RFID (IC tag), electronic money, SFA (Sales Force Automation), traceability, smart grid, CDN, AI (Artificial Intelligence), Crowdfunding
  - **(2) Software packages in typical business systems:** Software package for each job role (accounting, marketing support, sales management software), software package for each industry (finance, medical services, production, transportation), DTP (DeskTop Publishing)
  - **(3) Systems in other fields:** Basic Resident Register Network System, electronic application and notification system, CTI (Computer Telephony Integration), electronic bidding, Social Security and Tax Number

#### Item 15: Engineering system
- **Goal:** Understand the characteristics of typical systems in the engineering field.
- **Key Concepts:**
  - **(1) IT utilization in the engineering field:** Support of design and production by automation, promotion of efficiency
  - **(2) Typical engineering systems:** CAD (Computer Aided Design), CAM (Computer Aided Manufacturing), CIM (Computer-Integrated Manufacturing), concurrent engineering, simulation, sensing technology, production method, JIT (Just In Time), FMS (Flexible Manufacturing System), MRP (Material Requirements Planning)

#### Item 16: e-business
- **Goal:** Understand electronic commerce and the characteristics of typical systems.
- **Key Concepts:**
  - **(1) Electronic commerce**
    - **(a) The characteristics of electronic commerce:** Long tail, catalog sales
    - **(b) The classifications of electronic commerce:** EC (e-commerce), B2B, B2C, C2C, O2O (Online to Offline), EDI (Electronic Data Interchange)
    - **(c) Using electronic commerce:** Electronic marketplace, online mall, electronic auction, Internet advertising, Internet banking, Internet trading, SEO (Search Engine Optimization), affiliate, escrow service, opt-in mail advertisement, banner advertisement, recommendation, digital signage
  - **(2) Points to note in electronic commerce:** Security measures

#### Item 17: Consumer appliances and industrial devices
- **Goal:** Understand the concepts of embedded systems and typical examples.
- **Key Concepts:**
  - **(1) Embedded systems:** Firmware, robotics
  - **(2) Consumer appliances and industrial devices**
  - **(3) Examples of embedded systems**
    - **(a) Consumer appliances:** Rice cookers, washing machines, air-conditioners, cell phones, personal digital assistant
    - **(b) Industrial devices:** Industrial robots, automatic warehouse systems, vending machines, ATM (Automatic Teller Machine)

  - **(1) The types of information security measures:** Human, technical, and physical securities
    - **(a) The types of human security measures:** Information security policy, CSIRT (Computer Security Incident Response Team), access privilege
    - **(b) The types of technical security measures:** Encryption, authentication technology, ID, password, content filter, callback, access control, firewall, quarantine network, DMZ (demilitarized zone), SSL (Secure Sockets Layer), VPN (Virtual Private Network), digital signature, digital watermark, digital forensics, penetration test, one-time password, single sign-on, antivirus software, update of virus definition files, OS update
    - **(c) The types of physical security measures:** Biometric authentication, surveillance camera, locking management, entrance access control, ID cards
  - **(2) Encryption technology:** Common key cryptography, public key cryptography, encryption, decryption, WPA2
  - **(3) Public key infrastructure:** PKI (Public Key Infrastructure)
